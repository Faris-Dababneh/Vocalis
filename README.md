# Vocalis

### AI-Powered Social Anxiety Therapy for Gen Z

**Live Demo:** https://dist-one-tawny-90.vercel.app

## The Problem

Social anxiety is quietly one of the most debilitating mental health challenges facing young people today, and it is almost entirely invisible.

* **1 in 8 people** globally live with an anxiety disorder, making it the most common mental health condition on the planet (WHO, 2023)
* Social anxiety disorder specifically affects **15 million Americans**, roughly 7% of the population
* **Gen Z is the most anxious generation ever recorded.** 61% of Gen Z report significant anxiety symptoms, compared to 38% of Millennials at the same age (American Psychological Association, 2023)
* The average person waits **11 years** between the onset of social anxiety symptoms and seeking professional treatment
* **Only 5% of people** with social anxiety disorder ever receive evidence-based therapy
* The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the crisis. Three years of reduced in-person interaction left an entire generation significantly less practiced at basic social skills
* Social anxiety costs the US economy an estimated **$23 billion per year** in lost productivity

The tragedy is not just the suffering. It is that social anxiety is one of the most treatable mental health conditions we know of. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) with graduated exposure therapy has a success rate of over 80% in clinical trials. Yet the barriers to accessing it remain enormous: cost, stigma, waitlists, and the sheer difficulty of asking for help when your anxiety is about being judged by other people.

**Vocalis exists to close that gap.**

## What Vocalis Is

Vocalis is a mobile-first therapy companion that puts evidence-based graduated exposure therapy in the pocket of anyone with a phone.

It is not a meditation app. It is not a mood journal. It is structured, clinical, AI-personalized therapy, delivered in a format Gen Z actually wants to engage with.

Users complete a validated anxiety assessment, set their personal goal, and receive a fully personalized 20-challenge exposure therapy plan generated by AI in under 60 seconds. Each challenge is calibrated to their exact anxiety severity, their personal goal, and their timeframe. They complete challenges in the real world, reflect on their experience, track their progress, and gradually push the boundary of what feels possible.

The core insight: **anxiety shrinks when you act in spite of it, repeatedly, in a safe and structured way.** Vocalis makes that process accessible, measurable, and even enjoyable.

## How It Works

### Step 1: Assessment
Users complete a 12-question anxiety assessment based on the Liebowitz Social Anxiety Scale (LSAS), the gold-standard clinical instrument used by therapists worldwide. The app scores their anxiety from 0 to 72 across five severity levels: minimal, mild, moderate, marked, and severe.

### Step 2: Goal Setting
Users define what they actually want from their life. Not a clinical outcome. A real human goal: making friends at university, speaking up in meetings, going on dates, performing in public. This goal anchors every challenge the AI generates.

### Step 3: AI Plan Generation
Qwen AI generates a fully personalized 20-challenge exposure hierarchy in seconds. The plan is graded from the easiest possible starting point (making eye contact in a mirror, texting one person) all the way to advanced social challenges that push genuine growth. Every challenge is calibrated to the user's exact anxiety score, personal goal, and timeframe. No two plans are the same.

### Step 4: The Journey
Challenges are laid out as a visual journey path. Users unlock each challenge by completing the previous one. Each challenge includes:
* A clear description of what to do
* Three CBT-grounded tips written by the AI
* A personal encouragement message referencing their goal
* An XP reward and estimated time

### Step 5: Completion and Reflection
When a user attempts a challenge, they:
1. Rate their anxiety before attempting it
2. Optionally record a voice memo of their experience
3. Rate their anxiety after completing it
4. Earn XP and unlock the next challenge

This before/after anxiety tracking is clinically significant. Exposure therapy works because the brain learns that feared situations are survivable. Seeing your own anxiety score drop after doing something scary is powerful evidence.

### Step 6: Social Moments
Between scheduled challenges, users can log real-world social interactions as they happen using the Social Moment feature. They select their context (coffee shop, work, party, transit), choose a micro-challenge from a curated list, optionally record a voice reflection, and rate their confidence. Each logged moment earns XP and reinforces the habit of noticing and celebrating small social wins.

### Box Breathing
The home screen features a guided box breathing tool. A 4-4-4-4 breathing pattern (inhale, hold, exhale, hold) is clinically proven to activate the parasympathetic nervous system and reduce acute anxiety within minutes. Users can run sessions of 1, 2, 3, or 5 minutes before entering a social situation.

## Key Features

**AI-Personalized Therapy Plan**
Every user gets a unique 20-challenge exposure hierarchy built specifically for their anxiety score, goal, and timeframe. Powered by Qwen AI via Alibaba Cloud DashScope.

**Validated Assessment**
12-question LSAS-based assessment with clinical scoring and severity classification. Used by therapists worldwide.

**Graduated Exposure Journey**
Visual challenge path with sequential unlocking. Challenges span five difficulty levels: micro, easy, medium, hard, and elite. Each builds on the last.

**Voice Reflection Recording**
Users can record voice memos during challenge attempts. Hearing yourself describe a feared situation from the other side of it is a therapeutic tool in its own right.

**Gamification**
XP rewards, level progression, daily streaks, and achievement tracking. The same mechanisms that make games compelling, applied to building real social skills.

**Box Breathing Tool**
Built-in guided breathing sessions with animated orb. Clinically grounded anxiety regulation before social situations.

**Social Moment Logging**
Log real-world social interactions on demand with context-specific micro-challenges, voice recording, and confidence ratings.

**Guest Mode**
Users can explore the full app without creating an account. No friction barrier to getting started.

**Progress Tracking**
Full profile with XP bar, level title, streak counter, completed challenge count, and anxiety score history.


No tab navigators. One flat native stack. Every screen is always accessible by navigation name.

### AI Challenge Generation

The app calls Qwen with a carefully engineered system prompt and user prompt that includes the user's LSAS score, severity level, personal goal, and timeframe. The model returns exactly 20 challenge objects as a raw JSON array. Each object includes title, description, emoji, three tips, difficulty, XP reward, estimated time, encouragement, category, week, and day. The entire plan is written to Firestore in a single batched write.

The prompt enforces clinical validity: the exposure hierarchy must be graded, challenges 1 to 3 must be completable today indoors within 5 minutes, and encouragement must reference the user's personal goal.

### Firestore Data Model

```
/users/{uid}
  uid, email, displayName
  assessmentCompleted, onboardingCompleted
  anxietyScore (0-72), anxietyLevel
  goal, timeframe
  xp, level, streak, lastActiveDate
  achievements, planGenerated, planGeneratedAt

/users/{uid}/challenges/{id}
  title, description, emoji, tips[]
  difficulty, xpReward, estimatedMinutes
  encouragement, category
  status: available | locked | completed | future
  order, x, y, week, day, aiGenerated

/users/{uid}/completions/{id}
  anxietyBefore, anxietyAfter, hasRecording, xpEarned, completedAt

/users/{uid}/socialMoments/{id}
  context, mission, confidence, recordingUri?, xpEarned, completedAt
```

## The Clinical Foundation

Vocalis is built on Graduated Exposure Therapy, a component of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. It is not an alternative medicine approach or a wellness trend. It is the same framework used in clinical psychology for the past 40 years, with an overwhelming evidence base.

The core mechanism: when a person repeatedly encounters a feared situation without the catastrophic outcome they anticipated, the brain's threat-detection system (the amygdala) gradually reduces its alarm response. This is called habituation. The key word is "gradually." Moving too fast creates re-traumatisation. Moving too slow creates avoidance. A personalised hierarchy that matches the user's current anxiety level is what makes the difference.

The LSAS assessment that anchors the app was developed by Dr. Michael Liebowitz in 1987 and remains the most widely used clinician-administered instrument for social anxiety disorder. Vocalis uses a 12-question adaptation that maps to the same severity bands.

The before/after anxiety ratings after each challenge serve a specific clinical function: they provide the user with objective evidence that the feared situation was survivable and that their anxiety reduced. This is a cornerstone of CBT: gathering data to challenge catastrophic predictions.

## Design Philosophy

The app follows a Headspace-inspired light design language: cream and white backgrounds, slate text, and a calming blue and purple primary palette. The goal is to feel like a trusted companion rather than a clinical tool. The visual language should reduce, not amplify, anxiety.

Every copy decision avoids clinical jargon. "How anxious do you feel right now?" rather than "Rate your subjective units of distress." "Your mission" rather than "Exposure task." The therapy is rigorous. The language is human.

## Running the Project Locally

**Prerequisites:** Node.js 18+, Expo CLI, Firebase project, Alibaba Cloud DashScope account

**1. Clone and install**

```bash
git clone https://github.com/Faris-Dababneh/Vocalis.git
cd Vocalis
npm install
```

**2. Environment variables**

Create a `.env` file in the project root:

```
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_API_KEY=your_key
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_AUTH_DOMAIN=your_project.firebaseapp.com
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_PROJECT_ID=your_project_id
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_STORAGE_BUCKET=your_project.appspot.com
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_MESSAGING_SENDER_ID=your_sender_id
EXPO_PUBLIC_FIREBASE_APP_ID=your_app_id
EXPO_PUBLIC_DASHSCOPE_API_KEY=your_dashscope_key
```

**3. Start the development server**

```bash
npx expo start
```

**4. Deploy to web**

```bash
node_modules/.bin/expo export --platform web
npx vercel --prod dist/
```

## What Makes This Different

Most mental health apps fall into one of two categories: passive content (meditation guides, mood journals, breathing tools) or clinical intake portals. Neither actually changes behaviour.

Vocalis is built around the only thing that actually works for social anxiety: doing the thing you are afraid of, in a graded, supported way. The AI personalisation means users are not following a generic programme. They are following a plan built for their specific score, their specific goal, and their specific timeline.

The voice reflection feature adds a layer of therapeutic depth that most apps miss. Hearing yourself describe completing something you were afraid of, in your own voice, from the other side of the experience, is a form of self-reinforcement with genuine clinical grounding.

The gamification is not superficial. XP, levels, and streaks exist because behaviour change is hard and motivation is fragile. The same reward mechanisms that make games compelling can make therapy completion compelling too.

And critically: it works on any device, with no appointment, no waitlist, no insurance, and no co-pay. The barrier is zero.
